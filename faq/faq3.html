3. "I'd like to work with someone/need help on a project."
{

   Asking to work with others is great! But it’s important to be clear about
   the plans and what you’re offering. This makes it easier for others to
   decide whether they’re interested:


   Asking for an artist? Be clear about the amount of work, price per
   batch, and batch size.

   Need a programmer? State the hours required or price per task to make
   the offer more appealing.

   Need a musician? mention the genres and.. you get the point.

   Asking for free work? It’s okay, but be mindful that most people don’t
   want to work for exposure when they could do the same for themselves, let
   alone for actual money.

   It’s always helpful to give clear expectations.
   If you’re unsure what kind of offer to make, ask in
   a relevant community or channel what people typically expect to be paid for
   specific types of work. This could involve looking at commission sheets or
   discussing general pricing standards. Clear communication goes a long way!

}
